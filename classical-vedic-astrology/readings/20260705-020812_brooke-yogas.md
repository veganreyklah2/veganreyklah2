Saturn stands in the sign just ahead of her Moon, forming *Sunapha*, the yoga of self-earned standing — intelligence, reputation, and wealth acquired by one's own effort, wearing work clothes when Saturn is the maker: the rise comes through patience, discipline, and skill, and it stays. The same companionship settles the lonely-Moon question before it can be asked: *Kemadruma* requires a Moon with no planet beside, before, or behind it, and her Moon is companioned in both wheels. This heart travels accompanied. Her Sun keeps company too: Mercury shares its sign, forming *Budha-Aditya*, the union of light and intellect — keen mind, skillful speech, a good name — expressing cleanly with Mercury well clear of the beams, and seated in the partnership house it reads as intelligence spent on union. Mars holds the sign behind the Sun, forming *Vasi* — skillful, charitable, well-regarded, energy walking one step ahead of the light. And her bright, nearly full Moon attends the ascendant's forward flank, lending the grace of speech and bearing the manual promises when gentle planets attend the lagna. Four voices, one sentence: *she does her own rising, and she never rises alone.*
