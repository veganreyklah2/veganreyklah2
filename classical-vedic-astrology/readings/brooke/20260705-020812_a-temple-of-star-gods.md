Gathered together, her stars form a temple with every altar tended. At her door, her heart, and her essence-strength stands Vishnu: Uttara Ashadha, named among his own three stars, holds her ascendant, her Moon, and her essence-Mars — the later victory promised three times. At her light stands Brihaspati on Pushya the nourisher. At the skilled center of her essence stands Savitar, the golden-handed impeller, whose maker's gathering holds Moon in Chitra, Mercury in Hasta, and exalted Saturn in Hasta. At her essence-light keeps the deep serpent of Uttara Bhadrapada; at her mind and essence-release, Rudra's clearing storm of Ardra. At her love stands Bhaga, god of the hearth's happiness, who — when his own eyes were lost — saw thereafter through the eyes of friendship: fortune sees by love; at her essence-love ride the Ashwins, twin healers of the dawn. At her faith blows Vayu's free wind of Swati; at her depths keep Varuna's hundred medicines; at her creative release burns Agni's flame of Krittika; at her aspirations stand Vishakha's gateway-resolve and the invincible waters of Purva Ashadha; at her strength, Brahma's own Rohini, the creator's star; and coiled wise at the essence-door, the serpents of Ashlesha, who see in the dark. Whichever way she turns in her own chart, she is standing in front of an altar.
