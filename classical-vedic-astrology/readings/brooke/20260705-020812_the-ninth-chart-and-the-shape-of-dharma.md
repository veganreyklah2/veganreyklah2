Dharma is the essence of a thing — the quality so inseparable that removing it removes the thing itself. Wetness is the dharma of water; heat and brightness, of fire. The old word for wrongdoing meant simply to be *without* — outside one's own essence. Dharmas layer: incidental duties arrive with a season and leave with it, while the constants run beneath, and deepest of all runs the one dharma every being shares — the longing to experience the supreme, pursued *through* the roles the present season has given rather than apart from them. The householder's table is as true a temple as any cave, and the workshop bench belongs beside it on that list.

The main chart is the face of the clock; the ninth chart is the way the clock ticks — the *what* and the *why*, a hammer and a screwdriver rather than two hammers. Living in line with the ninth chart feels natural and sustainable. Laid back upon the main wheel, Brooke's essence answers with precision twice over. Her essence-Mars at 0°20′ Aquarius rests upon her natal Moon at 1°15′ — under one degree apart, the two sharing Uttara Ashadha, the star of the unbroken, righteous, later victory: her endurance runs deeper than habit; it is the essence reaching through, arriving exactly where her feelings labor. Her essence-Ketu rests upon her natal Mercury within two degrees — contemplative release laid directly on the mind. And carried back by sign, the essence tells one more secret: the maker's gathering — Hasta's word and discipline with Chitra's designing heart stands in Libra, and Libra is her tenth house. *The skilled hands work through the house of vocation.*
